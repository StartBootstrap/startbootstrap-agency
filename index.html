Preface.

This project belongs to the 'Knowledge Representation and Extraction' course of University of Bologna, a.y. 2018/2019.The project is aimed at extracting knowledge of lyrics of 35k rap song dating from 1980 - 2015 with the final goal to covert extracted data in a linked open data environment as the RDF graph.

 

Introduction.

The idea behind the project is inspired by the fact that rap is not just about music. Rap is an intensely intertextual and self-referential form. One of the reason for that is the extremely competitive expressive form of rap music. For that reason, emcees exhibit their rhyming abilities against the competition. Hip Hop is a unique era of culture where art, music, history, politics, fashion, trends and language became deeply pitted with metaphors that became consistent, ubiquitous and global. Hence, hip hop music is a milestone of information about culture at different time points.

Ontology Creation and Visualisation


An ontology is a detailed model of a slice of reality which is based on the facts that we know about that reality. This model is a description of some of the things and some of the relationships between the things that are known about that reality.

The RAP Ontology is intended to promote a shared understanding of rap lyrics as the source of cultural heritage information and to reuse the knowledge by providing a framework that any rap song can be mapped to. In this way, it helps to mediate between different sources of information on the Semantic Web.

While creating the ontology, the results of the lyrics mining was taken into account. Hence, 11 classes (superclass owl:Thing) were created: Album, Car, Genre, Literary Device, Person, Place, Release_Date, Sentiment, Topic. Considering the results of VADER Sentiment Analysis the following subclasses to class Sentiment were added:(subclasses: Compound, Negative, Neutral, Positive). The literary devices manual analysis resulted in the creation of 15 subclasses to the class Literary device:zeugma, tmesis, synesthesia, synecdoche, spoonerism, simile, portmanteau, polysyndeton, pleonasm, pangrammatic, palindrom, metonymy, litotes, irony, imagery.

Getting and cleaning the data

The wordcloud generated with online tool WordArt shows the most used car brands in lyrics.

Wikipedia's lists of car manufacturers and brands by country was manually downloaded. To avoid HTML parsing, Mobile View was selected. By running clean_carlist_wiki.py brand name tags were extracted and cleaned with Python Beautiful Soup library and NLTK.Click here for the final result hellacars.csv

By running reader1.py I obtained the total number of times the brand car names were used by each artist. 

To avoid disambiguation some car brands were not included in the lyrics mining since they have double meaning or unrelated proper names, e.g., Prince, MINI, Oakland.

Literary Devices

Manually analysing lyrics.

This part shows the result of manuall lyrics analysis that was aimed at detecting the use of literary devices and their interpretetion. I limited my analysis to 15 literary devices: zeugma, tmesis, synesthesia, synecdoche, spoonerism, simile, portmanteau, polysyndeton, pleonasm, pangrammatic, palindrom, metonymy, litotes, irony, imagery.

Definition

Zeugma

 

Zeugma is a figure of speech in which one word, usually a verb, is used to modify more than one noun. A zeugma is used to blend grammatically and logically different ideas.

 

He held her hand and his temper.

verb—->noun1——->noun2

His boat and his stomach sank.

noun1<——-noun2<–verb

Hip-Hop Example:

She broke my heart and my bank account

Im deep in a trance and I ain’t comin out

–Freaky Girl by Travis Porter

A broken heart and a broke bank account are two completely logically different things that are blended together by using the verb broke.

Tmesis

Tmesis involves the separation of the parts of compound word by intervening words.

I pull the shit to make you think I got a vote to settle,

Yelawolf is on a whole ‘nother level

–Yelawolf’s verse on Big KRIT’s song Fulla Shit

 

Synesthesia

 

Synesthesia is a technique that authors use to appeal to more than one sense (sight, smell, sound, taste, touch) at a time. Usually, a characteristic of one sensation is used to depict an aspect of a different sense.

Examples:

The wind was bitterly (taste) cold (touch) that night.

The bright (sight) piano (sound) played in the lobby.

Hip-Hop Example:

Hey, I’m new in town, I don’t know my way around

But I got some soft (touch) white (sight) that’s sure to come back brown

–1-900-Hustler by Jay Z

 

Synecdoche

When a part of something stands in for the whole thing OR the whole thing stands in for the part.

 

 

“Seattle won the baseball game.” (whole for the part)

“Those wheels are fire.” (part for the whole)

Hip Hop Example: Jay-Z, “Run This Town”

Spoonerism

A spoonerism is a play on words in which the sounds of two or more words are transposed.

 

Examples:

The Lord is a shoving leopard.

(The Lord is a loving shepherd)

It is kisstomary to cuss the bride.

(It is customary to kiss the bride)

Hip-Hop Example:

I’m getting heated, best I go and take a shower, spoonerisms big enough to shake a tower

–Spoonerised Minds by Chester P

 

simile 

A simile is where one thing is compared to another to help with describing something

Raekwon’s line from Protect Ya Neck:

Rhymes rugged and built like Schwarzenegger

 

Portmanteau 

Portmanteau is simply a blending of two or more words(including their sounds and meanings) into a singular new word.

Examples:

Smoke + Fog = Smog

Brother + Romance = Bromance

Hip-Hop Examples:

When these streets keep calling, heard it when I was sleep

That this Gay-Z and Cock-a-Fella Records wanted beef

–Ether by Nas

Gay + Jay-Z = Gay-Z

Cock + Roc-A-Fella = Cock-a-Fella

 

Polysyndeton 

Creating a list by adding a conjunction in between all the terms on the list. The opposite of asyndeton.

 

 

“Let the whitefolks have their money and power and segregation and sarcasm and big houses and schools and lawns like carpets, and books, and mostly–mostly–let them have their whiteness.” (Maya Angelou, I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings)

Hip Hop Example: Eminem, “Lose Yourself”

Pleonasm 

Pleonasm is a rhetorical device where one or more redundant words are used consecutively in a phrase to express an idea.

Examples:

He earned a doctorate degree.

She sat close to the burning fire.

The tuna fish sandwich was the most popular item on the menu.

 

Hip-Hop Example:

The reaper the only thing that can ease my soul freezing cold

Feeling like I was given life and if I take it at least I choose

–Rain Drops by Slaughterhouse

 

 

Pangrammatic 

A sentence (or verse, or song) that includes all the letters of the alphabet. Like palindromes, they’re hard to find in songs. They’re more of a language game. I’m sure there are some rappers out there that have hidden pangrams and palindromes in their work.

Palindrom

 

 

A word or phrase spelled the same backwards or forwards. Pretty hard to work into a song, but amazing if you see one!

Party trap

Metonymy

A metonymy is a word or phrase that is substituted for a more formal word or phrase to which it is related.

Examples:

The suits were at an important meeting.

The suits = business people

The pen is mightier than the sword.

Pen = written word

Sword = military power

Hip-Hop Example:

We don’t get down like them clown and the kids

I’m used to being indigent, who said it’s all about the Benjamins?

–Reality Check by Binary Star

The Benjamins = vast sums of money

 

Litotes 

Litotes are a figure of speech consisting of an understatement in which an affirmative is expressed via the negative of its contrary.

Examples:

Affirmative – He is smart.

Litote – He is not dumb.

Affirmative – She is ugly.

Litote – She is no beauty queen.

Hip-Hop example:

I got a Houston ho – nah she ain’t the sharpest knife

In the drawer but she a damn good booster though"

–Just Another Day by Lloyd Banks

 

Irony

 

“The use of irony in literature refers to playing around with words such that the meaning implied by a sentence or word is actually different from the literal meaning. Often irony is used to suggest the stark contrast of the literal meaning being put forth. The deeper, real layer of significance is revealed not by the words themselves but the situation and the context in which they are placed.”

An example is this line from Rap God by Eminem contrasts the literal meanings of the words living and killing.

“‘Made a livin' and killin' off it.”

 

Imagery

The author uses words and phrases to create “mental images” for the reader. Imagery helps the reader to visualize more realistically the author’s writings. 

The author uses words and phrases to create “mental images” for the reader. Imagery helps the reader to visualize more realistically the author’s writings. The usage of metaphors, allusions, descriptive words and similes amongst other literary forms in order to “tickle” and awaken the readers’ sensory perceptions is referred to as imagery. Imagery is not limited to only visual sensations, but also refers to igniting kinesthetic, olfactory, tactile, gustatory, thermal and auditory sensations as well.

Sentiment Analysis

VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner)

Sentiment Analysis is a sub-field of Natural Language Processing (NLP) that is aimed at identifying and extracting opinions within a given text based on the computational treatment of subjectivity in a text. In order to obrain a sentiment score, VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) a lexicon and rule-based sentiment analysis tool was used

VADER uses a combination of A sentiment lexicon is a list of lexical features (e.g., words) which are generally labelled according to their semantic orientation as either positive or negative (compound, neutral as well).
